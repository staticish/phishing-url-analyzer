📚 What you're learning

Lookalike domains are the #1 phishing trick: paypa1.com, micros0ft-support.com, or unicode homoglyphs (аpple.com with a Cyrillic “а” → punycode xn--…). Always read the domain right-to-left from the TLD.

The real domain is the bit just left of the TLD. In paypal.com.secure-verify.ru, the actual host is secure-verify.ru — not PayPal. Subdomains lie.

Email auth — SPF / DKIM / DMARC: SPF checks the sending IP is allowed; DKIM verifies a cryptographic signature; DMARC ties them to the visible From: and says what to do on failure. A dmarc=fail with a brand in the From line is a screaming red flag. Also watch display-name spoofing (“PayPal <noreply@evil.ru>”) and a Reply-To that differs from From.

Defender habit: hover, don't click; verify the registrable domain; treat urgency (“account suspended — act now”) as a manipulation signal.
